Recipe 01

Quick Juicy Smoked Pork Loin

This version prioritizes tenderness, moisture retention, and aromatic herb flavor with a lighter bark and gentle smoke profile.

At a Glance

  • Cook temperature: 235 to 245°F
  • Pull temperature: 142°F
  • Finish target: 145°F
  • Approximate cook time: 2.5 to 3.5 hours
Ingredients

Main Ingredients

Recommended

  • 1 whole pork loin
  • 6 parts kosher salt
  • 6 parts coarse black pepper
  • 4 parts onion powder
  • 4 parts paprika
  • 6 parts brown sugar
  • 6 parts dried rosemary
  • 2 parts dried thyme

Optional

  • 4 parts garlic powder
  • 1 part ground sage
  • 2 parts dried parsley
  • 1 part cinnamon
Equipment

What You Need

  • Offset smoker or side smoker
  • Charcoal
  • Instant-read or probe thermometer
  • Spray bottle
  • Foil or butcher paper
Preparation

Before the Smoke

  • Dry brine overnight with a light coating of kosher salt if desired.
  • Mix the recommended rub thoroughly in a bowl and add any optional flavor accents you want.
  • Optional binder: mix Dijon mustard and olive oil, coat the pork lightly, then apply the rub evenly.
  • Let the pork rest at room temperature while the smoker stabilizes.
Fire Setup

Smoke Profile

  • Maintain 235 to 245°F.
  • Use mostly apple wood with a small amount of red oak.
  • Aim for light, clean smoke and a gentle smoke flavor.
Cooking

On the Smoker

  • Place the loin away from direct heat with the thicker side toward the hotter side.
  • Insert the thermometer probe and smoke at 235 to 245°F.
  • Do not spritz during the first hour.
  • Optional moisture strategy: after the first hour, spritz every 30 to 40 minutes with apple juice, water, and a small splash of apple cider vinegar.
  • At about 130°F, optionally wrap loosely with butter and a rosemary sprig.
  • Remove from the smoker at 142°F so carryover cooking finishes near 145°F.
Finish

Rest and Serve

  • Rest wrapped for 20 to 30 minutes.
  • Slice across the grain.
  • Pair with herb potatoes, grilled asparagus, apple slaw, herb butter, or a light mustard cream sauce.

Notes: pork loin dries quickly above 150°F. Pulling early and maintaining clean smoke are the keys to this version.
