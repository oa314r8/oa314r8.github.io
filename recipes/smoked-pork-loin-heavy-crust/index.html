Recipe 02

Quick Smoked Pork Loin with Heavy Crust

This version is designed for deeper bark formation, a firmer exterior texture, and a more savory smoke profile while still keeping the pork loin juicy inside.

At a Glance

  • Cook temperature: 235 to 245°F
  • Pull temperature: 145°F
  • Approximate cook time: 2.5 to 3.5 hours
  • Wrap strategy: unwrapped for full cook
Ingredients

Main Ingredients

Recommended

  • 1 whole pork loin
  • 1 Tbsp kosher salt
  • 1 Tbsp coarse black pepper
  • 2 tsp onion powder
  • 2 tsp paprika
  • 1 tsp brown sugar
  • 1 tsp dried rosemary
  • 1 tsp dried thyme

Optional

  • 1 Tbsp yellow mustard or olive oil
  • 2 tsp garlic powder
  • 1/2 tsp ground sage
  • 1 tsp celery seed
  • 1 tsp crushed coriander
Equipment

What You Need

  • Offset smoker or side smoker
  • Charcoal
  • Instant-read or probe thermometer
  • Tongs
  • Optional spray bottle
Preparation

Before the Smoke

  • Dry brine overnight with a light coating of kosher salt if desired.
  • Mix the recommended rub thoroughly in a bowl and add any optional flavor accents you want.
  • Optional binder: coat the pork lightly with mustard or olive oil, then apply the rub generously.
  • Let the pork rest at room temperature while the smoker stabilizes.
Fire Setup

Smoke Profile

  • Maintain 235 to 245°F.
  • Use mostly apple wood with small additions of red oak.
  • Aim for thin blue smoke and clean combustion.
  • Avoid thick white smoke.
Cooking

On the Smoker

  • Place the loin away from direct heat with the thicker side toward the hotter side.
  • Insert the thermometer probe and cook at 235 to 245°F.
  • Leave unwrapped for the entire cook.
  • Use minimal spritzing. If desired, lightly spritz once after 90 minutes with water or diluted apple juice.
  • Cook until the internal temperature reaches 145°F.
Finish

Rest and Serve

  • Rest 20 minutes, loosely tented.
  • Avoid tightly wrapping so the bark stays intact.
  • Slice across the grain.
  • Pair with roasted potatoes, grilled vegetables, baked beans, mustard sauce, or herb butter.

Notes: start checking internal temperature early. Stable heat matters more than perfect precision in a small offset smoker.
