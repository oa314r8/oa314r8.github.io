# Quick Juicy Smoked Pork Loin

## Overview
This version prioritizes tenderness, moisture retention, and aromatic herb flavor with a lighter bark and gentle smoke profile.

---

# Ingredients

## Recommended
- 1 whole pork loin
- 1 Tbsp kosher salt
- 1 Tbsp coarse black pepper
- 2 tsp onion powder
- 2 tsp paprika
- 1 Tbsp brown sugar
- 1 Tbsp dried rosemary
- 1 tsp dried thyme

## Optional
- 1 Tbsp Dijon mustard
- 1 Tbsp olive oil
- 2 tsp garlic powder
- 1/2 tsp ground sage
- 1 tsp dried parsley
- 1/2 tsp cinnamon

---

# Equipment
- Offset smoker or side smoker
- Charcoal
- Instant-read thermometer or probe thermometer
- Spray bottle
- Foil or butcher paper

---

# Preparation

## 1. Dry Brine (Optional but Recommended)

The night before cooking:

- Pat the pork loin dry.
- Apply a light coating of kosher salt.
- Refrigerate uncovered overnight.

This improves moisture retention and seasoning penetration.

---

## 2. Prepare the Rub

Mix the recommended rub ingredients thoroughly in a bowl. Add any optional
flavor accents you want to include.

---

## 3. Apply Binder and Rub

- Optional binder: mix Dijon mustard and olive oil.
- Coat pork loin lightly if using the binder.
- Apply rub evenly on all sides.
- Let rest at room temperature while smoker stabilizes.

---

# Fire and Smoker Setup

## Target Temperature

Maintain:
- 235–245°F

## Smoke Profile

Use:
- Mostly apple wood
- Small amount of red oak

Aim for:
- light clean smoke
- gentle smoke flavor

---

# Cooking Instructions

## 1. Place Pork on Smoker

- Position loin away from direct heat.
- Place thicker side toward hotter side of smoker.

Insert thermometer probe.

---

## 2. Smoke the Pork

Cook at:
- 235–245°F

### Moisture Strategy

- Do not spritz during first hour.
- After first hour, spritz every 30–40 minutes.

Use:
- apple juice, water, and a small splash of apple cider vinegar

---

## 3. Optional Wrap

When internal temperature reaches about:
- 130°F

Wrap loosely in foil or butcher paper with:
- butter
- rosemary sprig

This helps preserve moisture and adds aromatic richness.

---

## 4. Monitor Internal Temperature

Remove from smoker at:
- 142°F

Carryover cooking should finish near:
- 145°F

Approximate cook time:
- 2.5 to 3.5 hours depending on smoker behavior.

---

# Resting

- Rest wrapped for 20–30 minutes.
- Keep loosely tented if using foil.

---

# Serving

Slice across the grain.

Excellent pairings:
- herb potatoes
- grilled asparagus
- apple slaw
- herb butter
- light mustard cream sauce

---

# Notes

- Pork loin dries quickly above 150°F.
- Pulling early is critical for tenderness.
- Maintain clean smoke rather than heavy smoke.
- Apple wood complements the herb profile exceptionally well.
