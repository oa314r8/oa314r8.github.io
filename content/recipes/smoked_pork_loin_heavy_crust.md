# Quick Smoked Pork Loin with Heavy Crust

## Overview
This version is designed for deeper bark formation, a firmer exterior texture, and a more savory smoke profile while still keeping the pork loin juicy inside.

---

# Ingredients

## Recommended
- 1 whole pork loin
- 1 Tbsp kosher salt
- 1 Tbsp coarse black pepper
- 2 tsp onion powder
- 2 tsp paprika
- 1 tsp brown sugar
- 1 tsp dried rosemary
- 1 tsp dried thyme

## Optional
- 1 Tbsp yellow mustard or olive oil
- 2 tsp garlic powder
- 1/2 tsp ground sage
- 1 tsp celery seed
- 1 tsp crushed coriander

---

# Equipment
- Offset smoker or side smoker
- Charcoal
- Instant-read thermometer or probe thermometer
- Tongs
- Small spray bottle (optional)

---

# Preparation

## 1. Dry Brine (Optional but Recommended)

The night before cooking:

- Pat the pork loin dry.
- Apply a light coating of kosher salt.
- Refrigerate uncovered overnight.

This improves moisture retention and bark development.

---

## 2. Prepare the Rub

Mix the recommended rub ingredients thoroughly in a bowl. Add any optional
flavor accents you want to include.

---

## 3. Apply Binder and Rub

- Optional binder: coat the pork loin lightly with mustard or olive oil.
- Apply the rub generously on all sides.
- Let rest at room temperature while the smoker stabilizes.

---

# Fire and Smoker Setup

## Target Temperature

Maintain:
- 235–245°F

## Smoke Profile

Use:
- Mostly apple wood
- Small additions of red oak

Aim for:
- thin blue smoke
- clean combustion

Avoid thick white smoke.

---

# Cooking Instructions

## 1. Place Pork on Smoker

- Position loin away from direct heat.
- Place thicker side toward hotter side of smoker.

Insert thermometer probe.

---

## 2. Smoke the Pork

Cook at:
- 235–245°F

### Bark Strategy

- Leave unwrapped for entire cook.
- Minimal spritzing.
- If desired, lightly spritz once at about 35–45 minutes using water or diluted apple juice.

---

## 3. Monitor Internal Temperature

Cook until internal temperature reaches:
- 145°F

Approximate cook time:
- 45 to 75 minutes for a typical 1.5 to 2 inch pork loin, depending on smoker behavior.

---

# Resting

- Rest 20 minutes loosely tented.
- Avoid tightly wrapping so bark stays intact.

Carryover cooking will raise internal temperature slightly.

---

# Serving

Slice across the grain.

Excellent pairings:
- roasted potatoes
- grilled vegetables
- baked beans
- mustard sauce
- herb butter

---

# Notes

- Small pork loins cook quickly. Start checking internal temperature around 45 minutes.
- Resist opening the smoker too often.
- Small offsets naturally fluctuate. A stable range matters more than perfect precision.
